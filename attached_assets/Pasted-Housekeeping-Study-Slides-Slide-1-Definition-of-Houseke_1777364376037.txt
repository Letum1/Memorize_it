Housekeeping Study Slides

Slide 1: Definition of Housekeeping

Refers to upkeep, cleanliness, and maintenance in houses or lodging establishments.

Examples: hotels, malls, schools, food chains, hospitals.

Slide 2: Types of Housekeeping

Domestic Housekeeping – Cleanliness in a house.

Institutional Housekeeping – Cleanliness in lodging establishments.

Slide 3: Organizational Chart

Executive Manager

Housekeeping Supervisor → Room Attendant, Chambermaid/Roomboy, Minibar Attendant

Public Area Superior → Gardener → Public Area Attendant (Lobby) → Pest Control

Linen Supervisor → Linen Attendant → Laundry Attendant → Iron/Steam Pressed

Slide 4: 5S in Housekeeping

Sort (Seiri) – Remove unnecessary items.

Set in Order (Seiton) – Arrange items for easy use.

Shine (Seiso) – Clean regularly.

Standardize (Seiketsu) – Create rules and routines.

Sustain (Shitsuke) – Maintain discipline and habits.

Slide 5: Check-Out Room Procedures

Check for lost items.

Pull curtains/blinds.

Adjust AC temperature.

Remove soiled dishes.

Empty ashtrays/waste.

Clean bathroom.

Wash glasses.

Clean/refill jug.

Strip bed linen.

Make bed.

Vacuum floor/carpet.

Dust furniture.

Polish mirrors/fixtures.

Replenish supplies.

Make up bathroom.

Fix curtains.

Check condition.

Close door.

Slide 6: Room Services

Make Up Room (MUR): 7–8 PM

Check Out: 11 AM–12 NN

Turn Down Service: 5–9 PM (focus on bed)

Slide 7: Turndown Service Procedure

Place cart.

Knock/activate doorbell.

Hang make-up sign.

Turn down bed.

Fold blanket corner.

Check curtains.

Refill jug.

Remove dishes.

Replenish towels.

Check garbage.

Empty/wash ashtrays.

Wash glasses.

Check bathroom.

Turn off lights.
